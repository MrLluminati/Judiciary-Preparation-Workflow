# Day 1 — Constitution — Article 12: State

## Session Status

Bare act reading completed for Articles 12, 13, 14, with skim of Articles 15 and 16.

## Priority

**Priority 1 — Must master.**

Article 12 is the gateway provision for Fundamental Rights. Before asking whether a Fundamental Right is violated, the first question is often whether the violator is “State” or an authority covered under Article 12.

## Bare Act Core

Article 12 says that, unless the context otherwise requires, “the State” includes:

1. the Government and Parliament of India;
2. the Government and Legislature of each State;
3. all local authorities within the territory of India;
4. all local authorities under the control of the Government of India;
5. other authorities within the territory of India or under the control of the Government of India.

## Plain Meaning

For Fundamental Rights, “State” does not mean only a State Government like Bihar or Uttar Pradesh. It includes the Union Government, State Governments, legislatures, local bodies, and other authorities that perform public functions or are under deep governmental control.

## Why Article 12 Matters

Most Fundamental Rights under Part III are enforceable primarily against the State. Therefore, Article 12 determines who can be directly challenged for violation of Fundamental Rights.

## Categories under Article 12

### 1. Government and Parliament of India

Includes the Union Executive and Parliament.

Examples:

- Central Government;
- Ministries;
- Parliament.

### 2. Government and Legislature of each State

Includes State executive and State legislature.

Examples:

- Government of Bihar;
- Bihar Legislative Assembly;
- Bihar Legislative Council.

### 3. Local Authorities

Includes bodies having local governance powers.

Examples:

- Municipal Corporation;
- Municipality;
- Panchayat;
- District Board;
- local development authorities, depending on statutory character.

### 4. Other Authorities

This is the most exam-relevant and case-law-heavy part.

It may include statutory bodies, corporations, agencies, or instrumentalities of the government if they are under deep and pervasive State control or perform public functions.

Examples may include:

- statutory corporations;
- government-controlled companies;
- public authorities;
- bodies substantially financed or controlled by the government.

## Key Tests for “Other Authorities”

Courts look at factors such as:

1. whether the body is created by statute;
2. whether it is substantially funded by the government;
3. whether the government has deep and pervasive control;
4. whether it performs public or governmental functions;
5. whether it enjoys monopoly or special status conferred by the State.

No single test is always conclusive. Courts look at the overall nature, control, and function of the body.

## Exam Examples

### Example 1

A State Transport Corporation cancels an employee’s appointment arbitrarily.

Likely Article 12 State? Yes, if it is a statutory/public corporation under government control.

### Example 2

A private shopkeeper refuses to sell goods to a customer.

Likely Article 12 State? No, because an ordinary private shopkeeper is not State.

### Example 3

A municipal corporation demolishes property without notice.

Likely Article 12 State? Yes. Municipality/local authority is covered.

## Prelims Angle

Possible MCQ traps:

- “State under Article 12 means only State Government.” — False.
- “Local authorities are included in State.” — True.
- “Every private body is State.” — False.
- “Other authority depends on control, funding, function, and statutory character.” — True.

## Mains Angle

Typical question:

> Explain the meaning of “State” under Article 12 of the Constitution of India.

Skeleton:

1. Introduction — Article 12 defines State for Part III.
2. Text — Union, States, local authorities, other authorities.
3. Purpose — determines against whom FRs can be enforced.
4. Other authorities — statutory bodies, instrumentalities, deep control, public function.
5. Examples — municipality, statutory corporation, government-controlled body.
6. Conclusion — Article 12 must be interpreted broadly to prevent the State from avoiding constitutional duties through agencies.

## One-Line Revision

Article 12 gives an inclusive and wide meaning to “State” for enforcing Fundamental Rights against the Union, States, local authorities, and other government-controlled/public authorities.

## Flashcards

Q. Is Article 12 exhaustive or inclusive?  
A. Inclusive.

Q. Are local authorities covered under Article 12?  
A. Yes.

Q. Are all private bodies covered under Article 12?  
A. No.

Q. What is the main test for other authorities?  
A. Government control, funding, statutory character, and public function are relevant factors.
