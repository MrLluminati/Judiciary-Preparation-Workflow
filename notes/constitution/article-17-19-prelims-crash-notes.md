## Purpose

These notes were prepared during the emergency prelims kickstart session after the prelims date was announced as **03.06.2026**. The focus is objective-exam recall, bare-act keywords, quick examples, and common mistakes.

# Article 17 — Abolition of Untouchability

## Core Rule

Article 17 abolishes untouchability and forbids its practice in any form.

The enforcement of any disability arising out of untouchability is an offence punishable in accordance with law.

## Key Words

- Untouchability
- Abolished
- Practice in any form forbidden
- Enforcement of disability
- Offence punishable by law

## Memory Line

Article 17 = untouchability abolished + practice forbidden + disability enforcement punishable.

## Common Mistake

Do not merely write that untouchability is discrimination or inequality. The sharper constitutional answer is that enforcement of disability arising out of untouchability is an offence punishable by law.

# Article 18 — Abolition of Titles

## Core Rule

Article 18 prohibits titles, except military and academic distinctions.

## Clause-wise Quick Map

| Clause | Rule |
|---|---|
| Article 18(1) | State shall not confer titles except military or academic distinctions. |
| Article 18(2) | Indian citizen shall not accept title from a foreign State. |
| Article 18(3) | Non-citizen holding office of profit or trust under the State shall not accept foreign title without President's consent. |
| Article 18(4) | Person holding office of profit or trust under the State shall not accept foreign present, emolument, or office without President's consent. |

## Meaning of Non-Citizen under Article 18(3)

A non-citizen means a person who is not a citizen of India.

Article 18(3) controls a non-citizen who is connected with the Indian State through an official position.

## Meaning of Office of Profit under the State

An office of profit under the State means a position under the government or State authority where the person receives salary, allowance, remuneration, or other financial benefit.

Example: a foreign expert appointed to a paid advisory post under the Government of India.

## Meaning of Office of Trust under the State

An office of trust under the State means a position involving public confidence, responsibility, or official duty under the State, even if salary is not the main feature.

Example: a foreign national appointed to a government advisory board or commission where official responsibility is involved.

## Memory Line

Article 18 = abolition of titles, except military and academic distinctions.

## Common Mistakes

1. Do not say Article 18 only applies to citizens. Some clauses also deal with non-citizens or persons holding office under the State.
2. Do not forget the President's consent requirement in Articles 18(3) and 18(4).
3. Do not forget that military and academic distinctions are exceptions.

# Article 19 — Six Freedoms

## Applicability

Article 19 applies only to citizens.

## Six Freedoms under Article 19(1)

| Clause | Freedom |
|---|---|
| Article 19(1)(a) | Freedom of speech and expression. |
| Article 19(1)(b) | Freedom to assemble peacefully and without arms. |
| Article 19(1)(c) | Freedom to form associations, unions, or co-operative societies. |
| Article 19(1)(d) | Freedom to move freely throughout the territory of India. |
| Article 19(1)(e) | Freedom to reside and settle in any part of India. |
| Article 19(1)(g) | Freedom to practise any profession, or to carry on any occupation, trade or business. |

## Removed Clause

Article 19(1)(f), the right to property, was omitted. Property is now protected under Article 300A as a constitutional/legal right, not as a Fundamental Right.

## Reasonable Restrictions

Article 19 freedoms are not absolute. They are subject to reasonable restrictions under Articles 19(2) to 19(6).

| Freedom | Restriction Clause | Grounds / Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Speech and expression | Article 19(2) | Security of State, public order, decency, morality, defamation, contempt of court, incitement to offence, sovereignty and integrity of India. |
| Peaceful assembly | Article 19(3) | Public order, sovereignty and integrity of India. |
| Association / union / co-operative society | Article 19(4) | Public order, morality, sovereignty and integrity of India. |
| Movement | Article 19(5) | Interest of general public, protection of Scheduled Tribes. |
| Residence and settlement | Article 19(5) | Interest of general public, protection of Scheduled Tribes. |
| Profession / occupation / trade / business | Article 19(6) | General public interest, professional qualifications, State monopoly. |

## Memory Trick

S-A-A-M-R-P

Speech, Assembly, Association, Movement, Residence, Profession.

## Memory Line

Article 19 = six freedoms for citizens, subject to reasonable restrictions.

# Article 19(2) — Obscenity / Decency / Morality Doubts

## Is obscene material defined in Article 19?

No. Article 19 itself does not define obscenity.

Article 19(2) permits reasonable restrictions on freedom of speech and expression in the interest of **decency or morality**.

The meaning of obscenity is developed through statutory law and judicial interpretation.

## Exam-Safe Point

Article 19(2) gives the constitutional ground of decency or morality. Whether material is obscene depends on statutory provisions and case law.

## Revealing / Semi-Nude Content Question

Revealing clothes or partial nudity is not automatically obscene merely because body is visible. The legal test depends on context, purpose, presentation, sexual explicitness, prurient appeal, and the overall effect of the material.

Examples:

1. Fitness, fashion, modelling, medical, artistic, or body-positivity content may not automatically be obscene.
2. Content presented mainly for sexual arousal, with explicit sexual focus, may cross into obscenity or sexually explicit material depending on the facts.
3. Content involving minors, non-consensual images, or explicit sexual conduct carries much higher legal risk under separate statutes.

## Prelims-Safe Memory Line

Article 19(2) = decency/morality is the constitutional ground; obscenity is judged by statutory law and case law, not by mere exposure alone.

# Common Mistakes from Drill

1. Do not confuse Article 19(1)(d) with Article 19(1)(e).
   - Article 19(1)(d) = movement throughout India.
   - Article 19(1)(e) = residence and settlement.
2. Article 19(1)(g) is not residence/settlement. It is profession, occupation, trade or business.
3. Article 19(1)(f) was right to property, but it has been omitted.
4. Property is now under Article 300A.
5. Article 19 freedoms are not absolute.
6. Restrictions on speech and expression are under Article 19(2).
