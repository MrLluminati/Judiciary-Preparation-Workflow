# Day 2 — Constitution — Article 15(2) Drill

## Study Label

Week 1 — Day 2 — Tuesday — 12.05.2026

## Topic

Article 15(2) — access to shops, public restaurants, hotels, places of public entertainment, wells, tanks, bathing ghats, roads, and places of public resort.

## Learner Answers and Feedback

### Q1. Article 15(2) applies against State only, or also against private persons?

Learner answer:

> Article 15(2) applies to State for Citizen.

Feedback:

Partly incorrect. Article 15(1) is directed against the State. Article 15(2) has wider operation and can apply against private individuals also, because it prohibits subjecting citizens to disability, liability, restriction, or condition in relation to access to specified public places/facilities.

Correct answer:

> Article 15(2) applies not only against the State but also against private persons in respect of access to specified public places and facilities.

---

### Q2. What places/things are mentioned in Article 15(2)?

Learner answer:

> Wells, Public Places, parks, etc which are maintained by the State for the use of Public.

Feedback:

Partly correct. Wells and public places are relevant, but the exact bare-act categories should be remembered. Article 15(2) mentions:

1. shops;
2. public restaurants;
3. hotels;
4. places of public entertainment;
5. wells;
6. tanks;
7. bathing ghats;
8. roads;
9. places of public resort maintained wholly or partly out of State funds or dedicated to the use of the general public.

Correct answer:

> Article 15(2) covers access to shops, public restaurants, hotels, places of public entertainment, and the use of wells, tanks, bathing ghats, roads, and places of public resort maintained by State funds or dedicated to the general public.

---

### Q3. Give one simple example of Article 15(2) violation.

Learner answer:

> A park maintained by Municipality of Patna restricts muslims from using the park. This is a clear violation on the ground of Religion.

Feedback:

Correct example. Since the park is a place of public resort maintained by a municipality, denial of access to Muslims on the ground of religion would violate Article 15(2). It would also raise Article 14 issues.

Refined answer:

> If a municipality-maintained public park denies entry to Muslims only on the ground of religion, it violates Article 15(2), because citizens cannot be denied access to public places or public resorts on grounds only of religion, race, caste, sex, place of birth, or any of them.

## Key Correction

Article 15(2) is important because it has a horizontal element. Unlike Article 15(1), which is against the State, Article 15(2) can operate against private persons also in relation to access to specified public places/facilities.
