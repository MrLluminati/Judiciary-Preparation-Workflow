# Day 2 — Constitution — Reasonable Classification Drill: Free Legal Aid

## Topic

Article 14 — reasonable classification: intelligible differentia and rational nexus.

## Fact Pattern

A law says only people earning below ₹3 lakh per year will receive free legal aid.

## Learner Answers

### Q1. What is the intelligible differentia?

Learner answer:

> People earning below Rs. 3 lakh per year.

Feedback:

Correct. The classification is between persons earning below ₹3 lakh per year and persons earning above that threshold.

### Q2. What is the object of the law?

Learner answer:

> To provide free legal aid so that it'll support the people under this group, financially.

Feedback:

Correct direction. Refine: the object is to ensure access to justice and legal representation for economically weaker persons who may not be able to afford legal services.

### Q3. Is there rational nexus?

Learner answer:

> Yes, it is because the people falling under this are weak financially, and by giving free legal aid to this group will protect their right.

Feedback:

Correct. There is a logical connection between income-based classification and the object of making legal aid accessible to financially weaker persons.

## Exam-Ready Answer

> The classification is between persons earning below ₹3 lakh per year and persons earning above that income threshold. The object of the law is to provide access to justice and legal representation to economically weaker persons who cannot afford legal services. There is rational nexus because income level is logically connected with financial inability to obtain legal assistance. Therefore, the classification is reasonable and valid under Article 14.

## Refinement Points

1. Always state both groups in the classification.
2. State the object in constitutional/legal terms: access to justice, legal representation, protection of rights.
3. Rational nexus exists when the income threshold is logically connected with inability to afford legal services.
