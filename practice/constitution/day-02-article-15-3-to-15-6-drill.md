# Day 2 — Constitution — Article 15(3) to 15(6) Drill

## Topic

Article 15(3) to Article 15(6) — enabling provisions / affirmative provisions.

## Learner Answers and Feedback

### Q1. What does Article 15(3) allow?

Learner answer:

> Nothing shall stop state for making laws in support of Women and Children.

Feedback:

Correct. Improve the wording: Article 15(3) enables the State to make special provisions for women and children.

Refined answer:

> Article 15(3) allows the State to make special provisions for women and children, notwithstanding the general prohibition on discrimination under Article 15.

---

### Q2. What does Article 15(4) broadly allow?

Learner answer:

> Nothing shall stop state for making laws in support of socially and educationally backward classes of Citizens & SC/ST.

Feedback:

Correct. Add the word “advancement” and keep the constitutional categories clear.

Refined answer:

> Article 15(4) allows the State to make special provisions for the advancement of socially and educationally backward classes of citizens, Scheduled Castes, and Scheduled Tribes.

---

### Q3. What does Article 15(5) mainly relate to?

Learner answer:

> Nothing shall stop state for making laws in support of socially and educationally backward classel of Citizens for admission in Educational bodies such as public or private institutions. Whether aided or non-aided by state. With exception to minority educational institutions.

Feedback:

Correct in substance. Improve the phrasing: Article 15(5) specifically relates to admission to educational institutions, including private aided or unaided institutions, except minority educational institutions under Article 30(1).

Refined answer:

> Article 15(5) allows the State to make special provisions, by law, for the advancement of socially and educationally backward classes of citizens, Scheduled Castes, and Scheduled Tribes in relation to admission to educational institutions, including private aided or unaided institutions, except minority educational institutions under Article 30(1).

---

### Q4. What does Article 15(6) relate to?

Learner answer:

> Nothing shall stop state from making special laws in support and upliftment of Economically weaker section similar to Article 15(4) & (5) but excluding the classes mentioned in it.

Feedback:

Correct. Article 15(6) relates to special provisions for economically weaker sections of citizens other than the classes covered by Article 15(4) and 15(5). It also permits reservation in educational institutions, subject to the constitutional ceiling stated in the clause.

Refined answer:

> Article 15(6) allows the State to make special provisions for economically weaker sections of citizens other than the classes mentioned in Articles 15(4) and 15(5), including reservation in educational institutions subject to the constitutional limit.

---

### Q5. Why are Article 15(3) to 15(6) not treated as unconstitutional discrimination?

Learner answer:

> Article 15(3) is for the betterment of Women and Children although it discriminates on ground of Sex. On the other hand, the 15(6) has a reasonable classification satisfying the object of law.

Feedback:

Partly correct, but incomplete. Article 15(3) to 15(6) are constitutionally permitted enabling provisions. They are not treated as unconstitutional discrimination because the Constitution itself permits special provisions for protected or disadvantaged groups to achieve substantive equality.

Refined answer:

> Article 15(3) to 15(6) are not unconstitutional discrimination because they are express enabling provisions under the Constitution. They permit protective and affirmative measures for women, children, socially and educationally backward classes, SCs, STs, and EWS in order to achieve substantive equality.

## Overall Assessment

Article 15(3) to 15(6) basic understanding: good.

Concepts understood:

- Article 15(3): women and children.
- Article 15(4): SEBC, SC, ST advancement.
- Article 15(5): educational admissions, including private aided/unaided institutions except minority institutions.
- Article 15(6): EWS special provisions/reservation.
- These clauses are enabling/affirmative provisions.

## Refinement Points

1. Use “special provisions”, not merely “laws in support”.
2. Use “advancement” for Article 15(4) and 15(5).
3. Article 15(5) should include SC/ST also, not only SEBC.
4. Article 15(6) applies to EWS other than classes covered by Article 15(4) and 15(5).
5. Article 15(3) to 15(6) are valid because the Constitution expressly permits protective and affirmative measures for substantive equality.
