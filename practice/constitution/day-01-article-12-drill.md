# Day 1 — Constitution — Article 12 Drill

## Learner Answers

### Q1. Why is Article 12 called a gateway provision for Fundamental Rights?

Learner answer:

> It distinguishes and defines the difference between a government body and a private body.

Feedback:

Good basic understanding. Refine for exam: Article 12 is called a gateway provision because it identifies the authorities against whom Fundamental Rights can generally be enforced under Part III.

Improved answer:

> Article 12 is a gateway provision because it defines “State” for Part III and determines whether a body can be directly challenged for violation of Fundamental Rights.

### Q2. Give three examples of “State” under Article 12.

Learner answer:

> Municipal Corporation of Delhi, Ministry of Commerce, and Bihar Legislative Council.

Feedback:

Correct examples.

Classification:

- Municipal Corporation of Delhi — local authority.
- Ministry of Commerce — Union Government authority.
- Bihar Legislative Council — State Legislature.

### Q3. Is a private school automatically “State” under Article 12? Why or why not?

Learner answer:

> No, it cannot be a State automatically, because it needs to be heavily government controlled.

Feedback:

Correct. Add that factors such as government funding, statutory control, public function, and deep/pervasive control may become relevant.

Improved answer:

> A private school is not automatically State merely because it performs an educational function. It may fall within Article 12 only if there is substantial government control, statutory character, funding, or public-law obligations sufficient to make it an instrumentality of the State.

### Q4. What does “other authorities” generally mean?

Learner answer:

> Any body / department that falls under the major control of Government.

Feedback:

Correct direction, but make it broader. “Other authorities” is not limited to departments. It can include authorities, corporations, bodies, agencies, and instrumentalities that are under government control or perform public functions.

Improved answer:

> “Other authorities” generally means bodies, agencies, statutory corporations, or instrumentalities which, though not strictly government departments, are under government control, perform public/public-law functions, or are substantially connected with the State.

## Error Log Points

No major error. Refinement required:

1. Gateway provision should be linked to enforcement of Fundamental Rights.
2. “Other authorities” should not be restricted to departments only.
3. For private bodies, apply control + funding + statutory character + public function tests.
