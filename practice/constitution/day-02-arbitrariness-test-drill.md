# Day 2 — Constitution — Arbitrariness Test Drill

## Topic

Article 14 — arbitrariness test.

## Definitions

### Arbitrary

Arbitrary means based on personal will, whim, preference, bias, or caprice rather than reason, rule, fairness, or objective criteria.

In constitutional law, an arbitrary State action is an action taken without rational basis, without fair procedure, without relevant criteria, or without proper reason.

### Arbitrariness

Arbitrariness is the quality of being unreasonable, unfair, irrational, unguided, or not based on relevant principles. It is the opposite of fairness, reasonableness, and equality.

Under Article 14, arbitrariness may violate equality because equality requires State action to be fair, reasonable, and non-discriminatory.

## Fact Pattern

A municipality gives shop licences to 20 applicants but rejects one applicant without giving any reason, even though he fulfilled all conditions.

## Learner Answers

### Q1. Is this a classification issue or arbitrariness issue?

Learner answer:

> Arbitrariness issue.

Feedback:

Correct. This is primarily an arbitrariness issue because the municipality treated one eligible applicant unfairly without reason or objective basis.

### Q2. Why may it violate Article 14?

Learner answer:

> The arbitrary decision of municipality, because it rejected one person without giving any reason even though he fulfilled all conditions to get license.

Feedback:

Correct. Improve by adding that a municipality is State/local authority under Article 12, and its action must be fair, reasonable, and non-arbitrary under Article 14.

## Exam-Ready Answer

> This is an arbitrariness issue rather than a pure classification issue. The municipality is a local authority and therefore State under Article 12. If the applicant fulfilled all conditions, rejection without reason, objective criteria, or fair procedure is arbitrary. Such arbitrary State action violates Article 14 because Article 14 prohibits not only unreasonable classification but also unfair and irrational State action.

## Refinement Points

1. Link municipality to Article 12.
2. Link arbitrary rejection to Article 14.
3. Use words: no reason, no objective criteria, unfair, irrational, unguided discretion.
4. Remember: Article 14 prohibits both unreasonable classification and arbitrariness.
