# Day 2 — Constitution — Reasonable Classification Drill

## Topic

Article 14 — reasonable classification: intelligible differentia and rational nexus.

## Fact Pattern

A law says only women employees will get maternity leave.

## Questions and Learner Answers

### Q1. What is the intelligible differentia?

Learner answer:

> Differentiation between women and men.

Feedback:

Correct. The clearer exam phrasing is: the classification is between women employees who may undergo pregnancy/childbirth and other employees.

### Q2. What is the object of the law?

Learner answer:

> Who can take maternity leave.

Feedback:

Partly correct, but this states the effect of the law rather than its object. The object is not merely to identify who can take leave. The object is to protect maternity, health, employment security, and welfare of women during pregnancy/childbirth and post-natal recovery.

Corrected answer:

> The object of the law is to protect the health, dignity, employment security, and welfare of women employees during pregnancy, childbirth, and post-natal recovery.

### Q3. Is there rational nexus?

Learner answer:

> Yes, only a women needs Maternity leave as she is the one pregnancy and needs care.

Feedback:

Correct. There is a logical connection between the classification and the object of maternity protection. The better wording is that the classification has rational nexus with the object because pregnancy and childbirth create biological and social needs requiring leave and protection.

## Exam-Ready Answer

> A maternity-leave law classifying women employees separately is generally valid under Article 14. The intelligible differentia is the distinction between women employees who may undergo pregnancy/childbirth and other employees. The object of the law is to protect maternity, health, dignity, and employment security during pregnancy and post-natal recovery. The differentia has rational nexus with the object because pregnancy and childbirth create special biological and welfare needs. Therefore, the classification is reasonable and not hit by Article 14.

## Error / Refinement Points

1. The object of the law must be stated as the purpose behind the law, not merely what the law does.
2. Intelligible differentia = basis of classification.
3. Rational nexus = logical connection between the classification and the object.
4. Reasonable classification is allowed; arbitrary classification is not.

## Current Status

Reasonable classification basic application: understood, with refinement needed in identifying the object of a law.
