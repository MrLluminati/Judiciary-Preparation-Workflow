# Day 3 — Constitution — Articles 14, 15, and 16 Comparison

## Study Label

Week 1 — Day 3 — Wednesday — 13.05.2026

## Question

What is the difference between Article 14, Article 15, and Article 16?

## Learner Answer

### Article 14

> Article 14 provides Equality before Law and Equal protection of Law. Meaning, each person is equal in the eyes of law. Law is above all. Any law made under this shall have a reasonable classification in order to treat each person of the state similarly. The state should not be bias, partial, arbitrary towards the people.

### Article 15

> Article 15 prohibits any discrimination on the basis of Religion, Race, Caste, Sex, and Place of Birth or any of them. It provides power to state to make any law in order to protect the rights or to prohibit / prevent discrimination to Women & Children under 15(3), Educationally Backward Section, SEBC, SC & ST under 15(4). It gives reservation to Educationally backward section, SEBC, SC & ST in any educational body being govt., private aided or unaided, with exception to educational institutions ran by minority under 15(5). It gives reservation to Economically Weaker Section in any educational body being govt., private aided or unaided, with exception to educational institutions ran by minority under 15(6)

### Article 16

> Article 16 grants equal opportunity to citizens in job appointments, promotion, exams, etc by prohibiting any descrimination on the grounds of Religion, Race, Caste, sex, descent, place of birth and residence or any of them.

## Feedback

The answer is substantially correct and shows good understanding of the relationship between Articles 14, 15, and 16. The learner correctly identified:

- Article 14 as the general equality provision;
- Article 15 as specific non-discrimination provision for citizens;
- Article 16 as equality of opportunity in public employment;
- reasonable classification and arbitrariness under Article 14;
- special provisions under Article 15;
- additional grounds of descent and residence under Article 16.

## Corrections and Refinements

### Article 14

The concept is correct. Refine the phrase “treat each person similarly” because Article 14 does not require identical treatment of all persons. It requires similarly situated persons to be treated alike and permits reasonable classification.

Refined version:

> Article 14 is a general equality provision. It guarantees equality before law and equal protection of laws to any person. It means no person is above law and similarly situated persons must be treated alike. Article 14 permits reasonable classification but prohibits class legislation and arbitrary State action.

### Article 15

Correct in substance. Refinements required:

1. Article 15 applies to citizens.
2. Article 15(4) covers socially and educationally backward classes of citizens, Scheduled Castes, and Scheduled Tribes.
3. Article 15(5) and 15(6) relate to admissions in educational institutions, including private aided or unaided institutions, except minority educational institutions under Article 30(1).
4. Article 15(6) relates to EWS other than the classes mentioned in Article 15(4) and 15(5).

Refined version:

> Article 15 is a specific non-discrimination provision applicable to citizens. Article 15(1) prohibits discrimination by the State on grounds only of religion, race, caste, sex, place of birth, or any of them. Article 15(2) protects access to specified public places and facilities. Article 15(3) to 15(6) are enabling provisions permitting special measures for women, children, SEBC, SC, ST, and EWS.

### Article 16

Correct. Refine by using the constitutional phrase “matters relating to employment or appointment to any office under the State.”

Refined version:

> Article 16 guarantees equality of opportunity to citizens in matters relating to employment or appointment to any office under the State. Article 16(2) prohibits discrimination in such employment on grounds only of religion, race, caste, sex, descent, place of birth, residence, or any of them.

## Exam-Ready Comparison

| Article | Nature | Beneficiary | Core Idea | Grounds / Scope |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Article 14 | General equality provision | Any person | Equality before law + equal protection of laws | Reasonable classification and non-arbitrariness |
| Article 15 | Specific non-discrimination provision | Citizens | No discrimination generally on specified grounds | Religion, race, caste, sex, place of birth; plus special provisions under 15(3)–15(6) |
| Article 16 | Equality in public employment | Citizens | Equal opportunity in public employment | Religion, race, caste, sex, descent, place of birth, residence |

## One-Line Memory

- Article 14 = general equality for any person.
- Article 15 = non-discrimination for citizens generally.
- Article 16 = equality of opportunity for citizens in public employment.
