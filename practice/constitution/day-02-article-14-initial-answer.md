# Day 2 — Constitution — Article 14 Initial Answer

## Question

What do you understand by “equality before law” and “equal protection of laws”?

## Learner Answer

> As per my understanding, "equality before law" means a citizen of India will be treated equally before the law. There will be no discrimination or biasness on the basis of class, etc. and "equal protection of laws" means, every citizen is protected under by the laws of India equally and there will be no discrimination based on class of citizen, but a reasonable classification will be there.

## Feedback

The answer is in the correct direction. The learner correctly understood that Article 14 is connected with equality, non-bias, and reasonable classification.

However, for exam accuracy, Article 14 should be framed as applying to **any person**, not merely citizens. Articles 15, 16, 19 etc. have citizen-specific elements, but Article 14 uses the word “person”. Therefore, foreigners and legal persons may also invoke Article 14 where applicable.

## Corrected Concept

### Equality before law

This is a negative concept. It means:

- no person is above the law;
- there should be no special privilege in favour of any person;
- every person is subject to the ordinary law of the land.

### Equal protection of laws

This is a positive concept. It means:

- equal treatment must be given to persons who are similarly situated;
- the State must protect persons equally through law;
- valid reasonable classification is allowed.

## Important Correction

Article 14 does not require identical treatment of everyone in all circumstances. It requires equal treatment of equals and permits reasonable classification.

## Exam-Ready Answer

> “Equality before law” means that no person is above the law and all persons are equally subject to the ordinary law of the land. It is a negative concept because it denies special privileges. “Equal protection of laws” means that persons similarly situated must be treated alike and protected equally by law. It is a positive concept and permits reasonable classification, provided the classification is based on intelligible differentia and has rational nexus with the object sought to be achieved.

## Refinement Points

1. Use “person”, not only “citizen”.
2. Equality before law = negative concept.
3. Equal protection of laws = positive concept.
4. Article 14 permits reasonable classification but prohibits class legislation and arbitrariness.
