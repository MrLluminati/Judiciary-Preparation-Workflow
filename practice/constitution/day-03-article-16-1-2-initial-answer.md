# Day 3 — Constitution — Article 16(1) and 16(2) Initial Answer

## Study Label

Week 1 — Day 3 — Wednesday — 13.05.2026

## Topic

Article 16(1) and Article 16(2) — equality of opportunity in public employment.

## Learner Answer

> Article 16 protects the rights of all the Citizen of India. Art. 16(1) protects their right for equal opportunity in any government exam conducted by the state. Art. 16(2) prohibits and sets the grounds for prohibition, that is Religion, Race, Caste, Sex, Descent, Residence, Place of Birth or any of them.

## Feedback

The answer is correct in substance. The learner correctly identified that Article 16 applies to citizens, that Article 16(1) deals with equality of opportunity, and that Article 16(2) contains prohibited grounds including descent and residence.

## Corrections and Refinements

### Article 16 applies to citizens

Correct. Article 16 applies to citizens, not every person.

Memory:

- Article 14: any person.
- Article 15: citizen.
- Article 16: citizen.

### Article 16(1)

The answer should not be limited only to government exams. Article 16(1) is broader. It covers equality of opportunity in matters relating to employment or appointment to any office under the State.

Refined answer:

> Article 16(1) guarantees equality of opportunity for all citizens in matters relating to employment or appointment to any office under the State.

### Article 16(2)

Correct grounds identified. The exact constitutional order is:

1. religion;
2. race;
3. caste;
4. sex;
5. descent;
6. place of birth;
7. residence;
8. or any of them.

Refined answer:

> Article 16(2) prohibits any citizen from being made ineligible for, or discriminated against, in respect of employment or office under the State on grounds only of religion, race, caste, sex, descent, place of birth, residence, or any of them.

## Error / Refinement Points

1. Do not restrict Article 16(1) to government exams only.
2. Use the broader phrase: “matters relating to employment or appointment to any office under the State.”
3. Memorise the exact Article 16(2) grounds in order, especially descent and residence.
