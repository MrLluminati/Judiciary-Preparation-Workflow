# Day 2 — Constitution — Article 15 Initial Drill

## Study Label

Week 1 — Day 2 — Tuesday — 12.05.2026

## Topic

Article 15 — prohibition of discrimination on grounds of religion, race, caste, sex, or place of birth.

## Learner Answers and Feedback

### Q1. Article 15 applies to citizen or person?

Learner answer:

> Citizen

Feedback:

Correct. Article 15 protects citizens, unlike Article 14 which applies to any person.

Refined answer:

> Article 15 applies to citizens.

---

### Q2. What are the prohibited grounds under Article 15(1)?

Learner answer:

> Religion, caste, race, sex, place of birth or any of them.

Feedback:

Correct. The exact constitutional order is: religion, race, caste, sex, place of birth, or any of them.

Refined answer:

> Article 15(1) prohibits discrimination against any citizen on grounds only of religion, race, caste, sex, place of birth, or any of them.

---

### Q3. What does “grounds only of” mean?

Learner answer:

> It means, only the grounds mentioned under Article 15.

Feedback:

Correct direction, but needs refinement. “Grounds only of” means that discrimination is prohibited when it is based solely on one or more of the listed grounds: religion, race, caste, sex, place of birth, or any of them. If a classification is based on another valid factor plus one of these grounds, the legal analysis may differ.

Refined answer:

> “Grounds only of” means that the State cannot discriminate against a citizen solely because of religion, race, caste, sex, place of birth, or any of them.
