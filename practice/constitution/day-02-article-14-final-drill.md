# Day 2 — Constitution — Article 14 Final Drill

## Study Label

Week 1 — Day 2 — Tuesday — 12.05.2026

## Topic

Article 14 — equality before law, equal protection of laws, reasonable classification, and arbitrariness.

## Learner Answers and Feedback

### Q1. Article 14 applies to “citizen” or “person”?

Learner answer:

> Article 14 applies to person.

Feedback:

Correct. Article 14 protects any person, not merely citizens.

Refined answer:

> Article 14 applies to any person within the territory of India, including citizens, non-citizens, and legal persons where applicable.

---

### Q2. What is the difference between equality before law and equal protection of laws?

Learner answer:

> "Equality before law" means the law is equal for every person in this country, irrespective of their caste, religion, designation, etc. It is also explained as Law above all, no person is above law. "Equal protection of laws" means there shall be no discrimination by the state for any person, everyone should be protected by the laws and same law applies on every person. Although, there can be classification on the basis of Reasonable Classification. The Reasonable Classification means classification on the grounds of Intelligible Differentia and Rational Nexus, and the state should be non-arbitrary towards a person.

Feedback:

Good answer. The learner correctly linked equality before law with “no person above law” and equal protection with equal protection by State action. Refinement needed: equal protection does not mean the same law must apply identically to everyone in all situations. It means persons similarly situated should be treated alike, and reasonable classification is permissible.

Refined answer:

> Equality before law is a negative concept. It means no person is above law and there shall be no special privilege. Equal protection of laws is a positive concept. It means persons similarly situated must be treated alike and protected equally by law. Article 14 permits reasonable classification but prohibits arbitrary and unreasonable classification.

---

### Q3. What are the two tests of reasonable classification?

Learner answer:

> The Reasonable Classification is based on two tests namely: a. "Intelligible Differentia" meaning there should be a difference between two groups of people. For example: Physically Disabled people and people with Non-Disability, or Men and Women, or Citizens above 60 years of Age to be considered as Senior Citizen and Citizens below 60 years of age. b. "Rational Nexus" meaning the difference/classification in question is logically reasonable in respect of the Law in question.

Feedback:

Correct. The examples are useful. Refinement: intelligible differentia is not merely “any difference”; it must be a clear and understandable basis distinguishing persons grouped together from those left out. Rational nexus means the basis of classification must have a logical connection with the object of the law.

Refined answer:

> Reasonable classification has two tests: first, the classification must be founded on an intelligible differentia which clearly distinguishes persons or things grouped together from those left out; second, the differentia must have a rational nexus with the object sought to be achieved by the law.

---

### Q4. What is arbitrariness under Article 14?

Learner answer:

> Arbitrariness means, a state shall not discriminate or deny protection of law to any person without giving a reason for rejection. For example: In a government job exam, there were 30 people shortlisted but 1 person was denied / rejected without getting any explanation or reason, which is arbitrary.

Feedback:

Good example. The answer is correct in substance. Refinement: arbitrariness is broader than rejection without reason. It includes State action based on whim, bias, unguided discretion, irrationality, unfairness, or absence of objective criteria.

Refined answer:

> Arbitrariness under Article 14 means State action that is unfair, irrational, unreasonable, biased, unguided, or without objective criteria. If an eligible person is rejected without reason or fair procedure, the action may be arbitrary and violative of Article 14.

---

### Q5. What is the difference between Article 14 and Article 15?

Learner answer:

> Article 14 is broad in nature, it covers every person, respectively Article 15 is specific in nature, it covers citizens of India. Article 14 speaks of prohibition of Arbitrary nature of law or state. Article 15 speaks about prohibition of discrimination on the grounds of caste, sex, religion, race, and place of birth.

Feedback:

Correct. Add that Article 14 is a general equality provision, while Article 15 is a specific non-discrimination provision applicable to citizens. Article 15 grounds should be stated as: religion, race, caste, sex, place of birth, or any of them.

Refined answer:

> Article 14 is a general equality provision applicable to any person and prohibits unreasonable classification and arbitrary State action. Article 15 is a specific non-discrimination provision applicable to citizens and prohibits discrimination on grounds only of religion, race, caste, sex, place of birth, or any of them, subject to constitutional exceptions.

## Overall Assessment

Article 14 basic understanding: good.

Concepts understood:

- Article 14 applies to person, not only citizen.
- Equality before law = no person above law.
- Equal protection of laws = similarly situated persons treated alike.
- Reasonable classification = intelligible differentia + rational nexus.
- Arbitrariness = unfair, irrational, unguided State action.
- Article 14 is broad; Article 15 is specific.

## Remaining Refinement Points

1. Do not say “same law applies to every person” too broadly. Say “persons similarly situated must be treated alike”.
2. Intelligible differentia must be clear and connected to the group distinction.
3. Rational nexus must connect the classification to the object of the law.
4. Arbitrariness is broader than rejection without reason; it includes unfairness, irrationality, bias, unguided discretion, and lack of objective criteria.
