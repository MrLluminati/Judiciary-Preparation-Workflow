Feedback:

Correct. Article 16(1) is broader than government examinations and includes eligibility, recruitment, selection, appointment, promotion, and employment-related matters.

Feedback:

Correct. Exact order: religion, race, caste, sex, descent, place of birth, residence, or any of them.

### Q5. What are the two extra grounds in Article 16(2) that are not expressly in Article 15?

Learner answer:

> Descent and Residence are two extra grounds in Article 16(2) that are not expressly in Article 15.

Feedback:

Correct.

### Q6. What is the difference between Article 15 and Article 16?

Learner answer:

> Article 15 protects the citizens from discrimination on the grounds of Religion, Race, Caste, Sex, and Place of Birth or any of them. Article 16 protects the citizens from discrimination in Government Employment or Appointment on the grounds of Religion, Race, Caste, Sex, Descent, Place of Birth and Residence or any of them.

Feedback:

Correct. Refined version:

> Article 15 is a general non-discrimination provision for citizens, while Article 16 is a specific equality provision for citizens in matters relating to public employment.

## Part 3 — Mains Skeleton

### Question

Explain the relationship between Articles 14, 15 and 16 of the Constitution of India.

### Learner Skeleton

#### 1. Introduction

> The relationship between Article 14, 15 and 16 is that they talk about Right to Equality.

#### 2. Article 14

> Article 14 is applicable on Any person. It treats any person as equal before law, and protects people from Arbitrary discrimination and allows Reasonable Classification on the basis of two tests, Intelligible Differentia and Rational Nexus.

#### 3. Article 15

> Article 15 protects citizens from discrimination on the grounds of Religion, Race, Caste, Sex, and Place of Birth or any of them. It enables State to implement laws for the protection of rights of Women and Children; to implement laws to advancement of any socially and Educationally backward classes(SEBC), SC & ST and give reservation in any educational institution except any minority educational institution under Article 30(1); to implement laws to advancement of Economically Weaker Section and give reservation in any educational institution except any minority educational institution under Article 30(1)

#### 4. Article 16

> Article 16 give equal opportunity to any Citizen for appointment or employment in any office of the State. It protects citizens from any discrimination in any office of the state on the grounds of Religion, Race, Caste, Sex, Descent, Place of Birth and Residence, or any of them.

#### 5. Relationship between the three

> All three talks about the Right to Equality, although, Article 14 applies to any person and the area of applicability is broader in nature, its concept is that everyone is equal before law, everyone should be equally protected by the law and reasonable classification should be allowed to protect the people, but there should not be any arbitrariness; and Article 15 applies to any citizen, it prevents discrimination on any grounds of Religion, Race, Caste, Sex and Place of Birth or any of them, it enables the State to implement laws to protect the rights and advancement of Women, Children, Socially and Educational Backward Citizen, SC and ST; and Article 16 also applies to the Citizen, although it protects the citizen from discrimination in any office of State for Appointment or Employment on the Grounds of any Religion, Race, Caste, Sex, Descent, Place of Birth and Residence or any of them.

## Refined Exam Skeleton

1. **Introduction:** Articles 14, 15 and 16 form part of the constitutional scheme of equality. Article 14 is the general equality provision, Article 15 prohibits discrimination against citizens on specified grounds, and Article 16 ensures equality of opportunity in public employment.

2. **Article 14:** Article 14 applies to any person. It guarantees equality before law and equal protection of laws. It permits reasonable classification based on intelligible differentia and rational nexus, but prohibits class legislation and arbitrary State action.

3. **Article 15:** Article 15 applies to citizens. It prohibits discrimination on grounds only of religion, race, caste, sex, place of birth, or any of them. Clauses (3) to (6) permit special provisions for women, children, SEBC, SC, ST and EWS.

4. **Article 16:** Article 16 applies to citizens in matters relating to employment or appointment to any office under the State. Article 16(2) prohibits discrimination on grounds only of religion, race, caste, sex, descent, place of birth, residence, or any of them.

5. **Relationship between the three:** Article 14 is the general equality guarantee. Article 15 is a specific non-discrimination provision. Article 16 is a specific application of equality in public employment. All three must be read together as part of the equality code.

6. **Conclusion:** Therefore, Articles 14, 15 and 16 together ensure equality, non-discrimination and non-arbitrariness. They protect both formal equality and substantive equality by prohibiting unfair discrimination while allowing constitutionally valid affirmative action.

## Conclusion Formula to Memorise

> Broad guarantee + specific non-discrimination + employment equality = equality code.
