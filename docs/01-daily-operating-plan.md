## Study Block Planning Rule

At the start of every day, record the original plan for all blocks before starting study.

For every block, maintain these columns:

| Field | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Original Plan | What was planned for this block at the start of the day |
| Backlog Added | What was shifted into this block from an earlier block/day |
| Actual Completion | What was completed in reality |
| Carry Forward | What remains pending |
| Status | Not Started / In Progress / Closed / Carried Forward |

This distinguishes real progress from backlog absorption.

## Daily Minimum Targets

Every study day should include:

1. One core law topic.
2. One bare act revision.
3. 10 MCQs or one mains answer/skeleton.
4. 15-minute recap before sleep.
5. No zero-study day.

## Weekly Rhythm

The weekly rhythm must follow the Wall Planner while respecting the user’s preference for focused subject completion.

### Monday to Saturday

Primary focus:

- Core law subject as per the 12-week roadmap.
- Bare act every day.
- Priority 1 provisions before anything else.
- MCQs or one answer-writing task daily.

### Sunday Morning

Full revision:

- Revise all topics covered during the week.
- Clear unavoidable backlog only after the weekly revision is complete.
- Run D1/D7 revision items.

### Sunday Evening

Practice output:

- One mock test; or
- Two mains answers; or
- One PYQ set plus error-log update.

### Sunday Night

Planning:

- Update notebooks, progress tracker, Google Sheet dashboard.
- Freeze next week’s original block plans.

## FAQ / Doubt Recording Rule

Whenever an important conceptual doubt, discussion, practical example, or exam-relevant clarification arises during study, record it in the relevant subject notebook in a clean **FAQ-style format**.

This applies to:

1. doubts expressly asked by the user;
2. related questions likely to arise in future;
3. practical courtroom/service-law examples that make the concept clearer;
4. judiciary-exam answer-writing clarifications;
5. comparisons between similar provisions;
6. common traps, misconceptions, and terminology corrections.

The FAQ section should not copy the raw conversation. It should convert the discussion into polished notes, such as:

- question;
- short answer;
- detailed explanation;
- example;
- exam-safe memory line.

Example format:

```markdown
## FAQ / Concept Clarifications

### Q1. Does Article 16(4A) create automatic promotion for SC/ST employees?

No. Article 16(4A) is an enabling provision. It permits the State to provide reservation in promotion for SC/ST where constitutional conditions are satisfied, but it does not automatically promote every SC/ST employee.
```

This rule applies prospectively and also to important prior doubts already discussed during the active subject block.
